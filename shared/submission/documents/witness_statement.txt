STATEMENT

I, Michael Johnson, hereby provide the following statement regarding the motor vehicle accident I witnessed on November 10, 2025, at approximately 1:30 PM on I-95 Northbound near Mile Marker 42.

I was driving northbound on I-95 in the center lane. Traffic was heavy due to rush hour, and it was raining heavily. Visibility was reduced, and the road was very wet with standing water in some areas.

As we approached the MD-43 overpass, traffic began to slow for a red light. I noticed a silver Toyota Camry in the right lane ahead of me and slightly to my right. The Toyota came to a complete stop, as did I in the center lane. We were both stopped for several seconds waiting for the light to change.

I then saw a blue Honda Accord approaching from behind in the right lane. The Honda appeared to be traveling at normal highway speed and did not seem to be slowing down as quickly as it should have been. At the last moment, I heard the Honda's brakes squeal, but it was unable to stop in time.

The Honda struck the rear of the Toyota with significant force. I estimate the Honda was traveling approximately 15-20 mph at the moment of impact, based on the sound and the movement of the vehicles. The impact caused the Toyota to lurch forward about 2-3 feet but it did not strike any vehicle in front of it (there was adequate space).

Immediately after the collision, I turned on my hazard lights and pulled over to the right shoulder. I approached both vehicles to check on the drivers. The driver of the Toyota (later identified as John Smith) appeared shaken but stated he was okay, though he mentioned his neck hurt a little. The driver of the Honda (later identified as Jane Doe) was visibly upset and apologized repeatedly, stating she had looked down briefly and didn't see traffic had stopped.

I remained at the scene until Maryland State Police arrived (approximately 10 minutes later). I provided my contact information to both drivers and to Trooper Wilson (Badge #12345).
